Physical Science Unit 6: Forces

    A force is:

    A push or a pull one object exerts on another object

  • Measurements: Pounds(English, lb) and Newtons(Metric, N)

    The 6 types of forces:


  • Normal Force: The force an object exerts to prevent other objects from passing through it

  • Friction Force: The resitance against two objects moving past each other

  • Gravity/Weight: The force of attraction between two objects

  • Tension: A force exerited by a rope, cable or sting or anything rope-like

  • Applied Force: A force applied by a person or another object

  • Air Resistance: The frictional force exterted on anything moving through a fluid

Net Force

The net force of an object is the result of adding up all of the forces acting on said object

Newton's 1st law

    Defintion:


  • An object will stay in its current state unless acted upon by a net force > 0

  • If all forces acting on an object are balenced, they will stay in its current state

  • Inertia: The tendency for objects to resisit chages in motion

Newton's 2nd law

  • Explains what happens when an object's net forces doesn't equal 0

  • When an objects net force is greater than 0, the forces are unbalenced

  • When the forces are unbalenced, the object moves in the direction of the greater force

  • Force can be calculated with the equation Force = (Mass)(Acceleration)

  • More mass = Lower acceleration, Less mass = Higher acceleration

Newton's 3rd law

  • Every action also has a reaction pair, this is called an ACTION REACTION PAIR

  • These action and reaction pair help create motion and all throughout our lives

  • These pair cause motion and don't cancel becuase these pairs do not work on the same object

  • The action's force is equal and oppiste of the reaction's force, even if the mass of action's is larger than the reaction's mass or if the acceleration isnt equal
